Create a clean, textbook-quality engineering physics diagram in a minimalist technical illustration style.

Style Requirements:
- 2D vector line art only.
- White background.
- Strict monochrome palette (black, dark gray, and light gray only).
- Uniform line weight throughout the illustration.
- Crisp, high-contrast vector graphics with sharp edges.
- Flat illustration with absolutely no perspective distortion unless required by the geometry.
- No photorealism, painterly effects, sketch lines, textures, gradients, shadows, reflections, or lighting.
- No anti-aliasing artifacts or rough hand-drawn appearance.
- Geometric precision comparable to diagrams found in university physics and engineering textbooks.

Objects:
- Represent objects using simple geometric primitives (rectangles, circles, blocks, wedges, pulleys, points, etc.).
- Surfaces should be straight, clean, and mathematically precise.
- Use only minimal flat gray fills or light cross-hatching when necessary to distinguish overlapping objects.
- Maintain generous white space around all elements.

Vectors:
- Draw forces, velocities, accelerations, and other vectors as clean straight arrows.
- Use consistent arrowhead size and shape.
- Arrows should originate from the correct point of application and remain uncluttered.
- Avoid overlapping arrows whenever possible.

Labels:
- Use a clean sans-serif font similar to Arial or Helvetica.
- Keep text horizontal whenever possible.
- Labels should be legible, evenly spaced, and never overlap objects or vectors.
- Use standard scientific notation (m, v, a, T, N, W, g, θ, μ, etc.).
- Use subscripts when appropriate.

Composition:
- Focus only on the essential physical setup.
- Keep the diagram centered and balanced.
- Remove unnecessary visual detail.
- Prioritize clarity, readability, and instructional value over realism.
- The final image should resemble a figure from a modern undergraduate physics or engineering mechanics textbook, suitable for instructional use.
